# neuroscience past present and future

# neurons and the glia 
there are 85 billion neurons in the brain and similar number of glia cells. most cells range between 0.01 mm to 0.05 mm in diameter  

rough breakdown of a cell:  
cytosol: potassium rich solution  
DNA: made up of genes where each gene encodes a protein. DNA is transcripted into mRNA to go outside. human DNA has 19000 genes.  
ribosome: translated mRNA into amino acids which make proteins. free ribosome make internal protein and rough ER make protein to be shipped outside  
smooth ER:lipid synthesis and detoxification  
golgi apparatus: post processes and ships proteins  
mitochondria: the power house of the ... neuron. makes ATP  
neuronal membrane: barrier that has protein pumps  
cytoskeleton: changeble scaffold that maintains structure. includes microtubles and microfiliments and neurofilaments.  
axons: output of a neuron, long segment. could branch at axon collaterals. sometimes recurrent collaterals send signal back.  
axon terminal: the part that contacts other neurons. short branches at the end called terminal arbor.  
dendrites: input to neuron, can form a tree. dendrites can have protruding dendrite spines that usually contacts axon terminals.  
synapse: where neurons communicate where axon terminal communicates with dendrites  
neurites: a axon or dendrite is called a neurite used for counting  

neuron classification:  
by number of neurites: unipolar (just as support) bipolar (like a gate?) multipolar (proccessing)  
by denrite shape: stellate cells (star shaped round shaped) pyramidal cell (somata pyramid shaped dendrites form a directioned tree like branch)  
by connection: primary sensory neurons, motor neurons, interneurons  
by axon length: projection neurons (long axons to another part of the brain, usually pyramidal) local circuit neurons (axon to nearby, usually stellate)  
by physiological properties: i.e. outer membrane electric gen  
by gene expression: i.e. what neurotransmitter it produces  

glia cells:  
astrocytes: star-like.  small soma with many branches processes. regulate chemical content of outer space. can restrict neurotransmitters or release its own neurotransmitter. can also grow and eliminate synapses.  

myelinating glia: wrapped around axon to speed up signals, leaves gaps called node of ranvier
